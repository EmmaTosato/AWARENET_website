Interdisciplinary research that keeps humans in the loop

Mission

Consciousness has both levels (e.g., wakefulness, sleep) and contents (what we perceive). These dimensions have rarely been mapped together in the human brain. AWARENET builds a unified atlas of neural correlates—combining complexity for level and tonicity for content—from intracranial recordings. We align these maps with the brain’s large-scale organization and use lesion disconnectomes to predict how focal damage may alter awareness. Our goal is a practical, open tool to inform clinical decisions.

Mission

Two sides of awareness

Levels (Complexity)

How richly activity spreads after a small, controlled perturbation.

Contents (Tonicity)

How long responses persist when processing sensory input.

Together

Seeing both on the same cortical canvas reveals where level and content converge or diverge.

How we study it

SEEG + hdEEG

Precise intracranial recordings paired with scalp coverage enable brain-wide readouts of perturbational complexity.

No-report sensory mapping

Estimate tonicity across modalities without behavioral reports.

Common space

Warp both atlases onto functional and structural gradients from 7T HCP data to understand network context.

Workflow

Tasks at a glance

T1 — Complexity Atlas

Build a cortex-wide map of perturbational complexity (PCI-like) from SEEG-SPES with hdEEG.

T2 — Tonicity Atlas

Map sustained sensory responses across modalities and compare with complexity.

T3 — Network Organization

Warp both atlases onto cortical gradients (fMRI/dMRI) and study network coupling.

T4 — Predictive Tool

Use lesion disconnectomes to predict awareness-related deficits; release an open model and code.

Why it matters

  • A first unified atlas linking level and content of consciousness in humans.
  • Anticipate lesion-related awareness deficits before interventions.
  • Open resources to accelerate validation and clinical translation.

Collaboration & openness

We bring together IN-CNR, UNIPD, UNIMI, and clinical partners at Niguarda. Results, code, and atlases will be openly released to maximize impact and reproducibility.
