Mapping the neural architecture of consciousness

Mission

AWARENET aims to build an integrated atlas of consciousness, combining fine-grained intracranial data with large-scale brain organization to explain how awareness emerges and how it can be disrupted by brain lesions.

Using stereo-EEG recordings and stimulations from drug-resistant epilepsy patients, AWARENET links the level of consciousness (how awake or responsive the brain is) and the content of consciousness (what we perceive) within a common spatial framework.

Two complementary measures drive this mapping: the Perturbational Complexity Index (PCI), quantifying the level of consciousness through the brain’s response to stimulation, and the Tonicity index, describing the persistence and content of conscious perception.

This unified Awareness Atlas serves both fundamental science and clinical practice, supporting diagnosis and rehabilitation in conditions such as stroke, epilepsy, coma, and vegetative state.

Main challenges

  • No direct fMRI: Intracranial recordings (sEEG) provide high precision but lack functional connectivity data.
  • Multi-scale integration: Bridging local neural dynamics (PCI, tonicity) with large-scale cortical organization requires advanced modeling and alignment techniques.
  • Causality and translation: Testing how focal lesions alter consciousness, and extending this knowledge to clinical tools, remains a key conceptual and methodological goal.

Our dataset relies on intracranial recordings (sEEG) from drug-resistant epilepsy patients, providing high spatial and temporal resolution but no direct fMRI data.

How can we link functional connectivity with consciousness when no direct fMRI is available?

What we aim to do

AWARENET will:

Map consciousness networks

Identify brain regions supporting the level and content of consciousness through intracranial perturbations and recordings (sEEG).

Integrate normative gradients

Align intracranial maps with large-scale cortical gradients derived from normative fMRI/dMRI datasets (HCP 7T).

Test causal mechanisms

Study patients undergoing controlled thermocoagulation lesions to compare behavioral decline with disconnection-overlap in the atlas.

Deliver predictive tools

Develop models that forecast how specific lesions alter awareness, bridging basic and clinical neuroscience.
